Temo Rekhviashvili is a Georgian writer, playwright and actor working across literature, theatre and cinema. He is the author of the novel "Courier’s Tales" and the collection "Two Plays", and the co-screenwriter and lead actor of the film "Temo Re". His work has been published, staged and screened in Georgia and internationally.

NOVELLA

Courier’s Tales

Courier’s Tales is the debut novella by Temo Rekhviashvili. Told through the voice of a young actor who works as a courier, the book is composed of fragments from everyday life: deliveries, theatre rehearsals, friendships, debt, exhaustion, self-irony and the effort to survive.

Set mostly in contemporary Tbilisi, the novel moves through streets, staircases, rehearsal rooms and precarious jobs. In its final part, the story opens toward Germany: the narrator travels from Georgia to Berlin and then to Hamburg, where his desperate attempt to reach a Nick Cave concert turns into a comic and painful episode of disorientation, language failure, humiliation and desire.

At once local and European, Courier’s Tales is a portrait of a young man trying to find his place between work and art, survival and longing, the city he comes from and the places he imagines as escape.

Two Plays

Published by Sulakauri Publishing, 2025
A collection of two plays by Temo Rekhviashvili

Pathetic Monologues — Two Plays brings together two stage works by Temo Rekhviashvili, rooted in the language, contradictions and absurdities of contemporary Georgian life.

Pathetic Monologues is a satire of public speech, privilege, politics and self-importance. CO₂ is a darkly comic play about masculinity, alcoholism, failed intimacy and the emotional wreckage left inside domestic life.

Featured Film

Temo Re

Short film, 2025

Temo Re is a Georgian short film directed by Anka Gujabidze and adapted from Temo Rekhviashvili’s debut novel Courier’s Tales. Rekhviashvili co-wrote the film and plays the leading role.

The film follows Temo, a young actor who also works as a courier. Over the course of a single day, he travels across Tbilisi on a moped and performs in a suburban theatre in the evening. Poverty, injustice, corruption and alienation gradually turn into disturbing visions.

Constructed as a black-and-white photomontage, Temo Re combines fiction and documentary elements to create a fragmented portrait of contemporary Tbilisi.

About

About

Temo Rekhviashvili is a Georgian writer, playwright and actor working across literature, theatre and cinema.

He was born in 1993 and graduated from the Drama Faculty of Shota Rustaveli Theatre and Film Georgia State University in 2015.

He has worked as an independent freelance actor across several theatres. In 2016, he took part in the founding of Open Space for Experimental Art, and in 2019 became one of the co-founders of Theatre Company Haraki.

In 2017, together with Gvantsa Enukidze, he founded the secret poetry group Universally Accessible Dramatic Poetry. Since 2019, he has been a member of Temur Chkheidze’s Studio.

His debut short novel Courier’s Tales was published by Sulakauri Publishing in 2021 and received the SABA Literary Award for Best Debut in 2022.

He is also the author of Pathetic Monologues — Two Plays, published by Sulakauri Publishing in 2025. Courier’s Tales later inspired the short film Temo Re, directed by Anka Gujabidze, which Rekhviashvili co-wrote and in which he played the leading role.

His work is marked by directness, dark humour and close attention to people trying to survive without losing the ability to laugh, desire, remember and speak.
